About Me

I am currently a Lecturer in the Department of Software Engineering at Daffodil International University (DIU), Bangladesh. My research focuses on computer vision, pattern recognition, image and video processing, and medical image analysis, with a strong emphasis on applying computer vision and deep learning to solve real-world problems.

I have led and collaborated on multiple projects involving medical imaging, image forensics, generative AI, and low-level image analysis. Many of my works target healthcare and security related applications, aiming to develop AI solutions that are robust, explainable, and capable of performing reliably in safety-critical settings.

I am deeply motivated by the potential of AI to make a tangible impact on society, particularly in healthcare, where accurate and timely decision-making can save lives. My long-term academic goal is to advance the development of intelligent systems that can integrate seamlessly into practical workflows while maintaining transparency and trust.

Research Interests

My research focuses on building robust and reliable AI systems, with an emphasis on:

  • Computer Vision
  • Deep Learning
  • Image Processing
  • Machine Learning
  • Medical image analysis
  • Generative AI
  • Low-level image analysis

Within these areas, I focus on developing methods that combine high accuracy with interpretability, enabling models to be trusted in high-stakes environments. My research also explores bridging theory and practice, ensuring that AI-driven solutions are not only technically advanced but also deployable in real-world settings.

Background

I currently serve as a Lecturer at Daffodil International University, where I teach and supervise undergraduate research in artificial intelligence, deep learning, and computer vision. My work spans projects in medical imaging, AI-based forgery detection, and others. I have presented my research at international conferences and have collaborated with students and faculty to publish in peer-reviewed venues.

My principle is simple: Learn and Implement. This approach has driven my work from the classroom to the research lab, and it continues to shape my pursuit of impactful and innovative AI research.
